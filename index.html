EKG Digitization


The EKG Digitization project was the first step in predicting the results of Cardiac MRIs from EKGs. Cardiac MRIs can be expensive and time consuming. Predicting their results from EKGs will save time and money. The digitized EKGs will be used to train a convolutional neural network to predict a cardiac MRI from an EKG waveform. This project ensured that the pdf forms of the EKGs were digitized into a qualitative form that the CNN could be trained on.

Sickbay Data Extraction


The Sickbay Data Extraction project focused on extracting waveform data from the Sickbay platform for analysis. The purpose was to provide IBM with this data to train a convolutional neural network to predict Low Cardiac Output Syndrome (LCOS) in children with congenital heart disease (CHD). By developing algorithms to identify common waveforms and determine missing data, I ensured the extraction and delivery of complete datasets to IBM. The overarching goal is to leverage AI to predict LCOS, enabling timely medical interventions and improving patient outcomes.

Just A Drop


Just A Drop was a project dedicated to raising $4,500 to build a water well in Africa. I successfully fundraised the necessary amount through various means, including speaking at conferences, interviewing for news articles, and organizing raffles at school. After reaching the fundraising goal, I was given the opportunity to choose the well's location in Nigeria. To help as many people as possible, I wrote a Python script that used the haversine equation and longitude and latitude values from the simplemaps.com API to calculate the farthest distance from any existing water well to a city in Nigeria, ensuring the new well would have the maximum impact.
